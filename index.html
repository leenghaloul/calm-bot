Mental Health at Khalifa University

Mental health struggles among university students are often more common than people assume, yet many students still feel isolated or unsupported. This website was created to raise awareness about how mental health is experienced and perceived at Khalifa University, and why stronger student-centered support matters.

Research in the UAE has already shown that mental health concerns among university students are a serious issue, with one recent study reporting that almost half of surveyed students showed signs of moderate to severe depression. Other studies also suggest that students’ perceptions of how common mental health struggles are can influence stigma, loneliness, and willingness to seek help.

Why This Matters

Many students may be struggling silently. When emotional distress is misunderstood, underestimated, or treated as only an academic issue, students may avoid asking for help.

How the Research Was Done

This project was based on a survey of 69 Khalifa University undergraduate students. The survey used Likert-scale, yes/no, and multiple-choice questions to explore emotional strain, perceived mental health prevalence, and views on campus support.

What the Study Found

The results showed signs of emotional strain, low counselling use, and mixed perceptions of support. Together, these findings suggest that many students may need more effective emotional support on campus.

Key Findings

1. Emotional strain appears common

Students reported frequent sleep difficulty and irritation, while motivation levels were often moderate to low. This suggests that emotional strain may be affecting students even when it is not formally diagnosed.

Sleep difficulty: Mean = 3.52 / 5

Irritation: Mean = 3.34 / 5

Mood: Mean = 2.61 / 5

2. Students feel distress, but few use counselling

Although many respondents showed signs of emotional distress, only a small percentage had ever visited a campus counsellor. This may suggest that formal services are not reaching students effectively.

14.5%

visited a campus counsellor

49.3%

felt alone in their struggles

13.0%

reported an official diagnosis

3. Perceptions of mental health are mixed

Most students believed that a large percentage of KU students struggle with poor mental health, but estimates varied a lot. This suggests that awareness exists, but a clear shared understanding does not.

59.4% of respondents estimated that 40% to 80% of KU students struggle frequently with poor mental health.

What Should Change

Improve counselling services

Counselling should feel more focused on students’ emotional well-being, not just academic performance. Students need support that feels personal, trusted, and effective in the long term.

Train faculty better

Faculty members should receive more training on how to respond to students facing mental health difficulties, including how to refer them to support in a sensitive way.

Reduce stigma on campus

Awareness efforts should make it easier for students to talk openly about distress, loneliness, and help-seeking without feeling ashamed or judged.

About This Project

This website is based on an Academic English II technical report completed at Khalifa University. Its purpose is to turn academic research into a more public-facing message that is easier to understand and more useful for a wider audience.

The project was created to encourage more awareness, more honest conversations, and better mental health support for students on campus.
